02

Featured case study

GRC Risk Assessment · NIST CSF

Pinnacle: a risk assessment of a residential mortgage-lending workflow

A full governance, risk, and compliance assessment applying the NIST Cybersecurity Framework to a regulated lending process — the exact domain I've operated in for two decades.

The challenge

Residential mortgage lending moves high volumes of sensitive customer PII through CRM, origination, and document systems under GLBA, RESPA, TILA, and CFPB obligations. The objective: assess where that workflow was exposed and produce findings a compliance or examination team could act on.

Approach

  • Mapped the end-to-end lending workflow against the five NIST CSF functions — Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover.
  • Performed a controls gap analysis, identifying where existing safeguards fell short of framework expectations.
  • Documented risk-scored findings mapped directly to financial-services regulatory obligations.
  • Produced a prioritized remediation roadmap with audit-ready evidence and rationale.

Why it matters

This is not a generic lab. It is a security risk assessment of the precise environment I know at the operational level — which means the control gaps and regulatory mappings reflect how the work is actually done, not a textbook template.

Outcome

A complete GRC deliverable: risk register, gap analysis, and remediation roadmap — published to GitHub as evidence of applied capability in financial-services risk.
